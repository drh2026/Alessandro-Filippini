Alessandro Filippini

Alessandro Filippini served as maître d’hôtel at Delmonico’s in New York for over two decades, working under the legendary Charles Ranhofer. The restaurant was the most celebrated dining establishment in America during the Gilded Age, hosting presidents, heads of state, and society’s elite. Filippini’s meticulous knowledge of service, purchasing, and preparation made him one of the foremost authorities on American fine dining of his era.

The Book

The Table (1889) is a comprehensive guide to every aspect of fine dining—how to source ingredients from market, how to set and serve a table with proper Delmonico style, daily menus for every day of the year, and over 1,000 meticulously numbered recipes covering soups through desserts. It is a direct window into the kitchen of America’s greatest Gilded Age restaurant.

This Archive

This digital edition presents 1,1,034 recipes from the 1889 first edition, each linking directly to its page in the original scanned book on Archive.org. Categories follow Filippini’s own chapter structure, from Soups (No. 1) through Desserts (No. 1,200).
